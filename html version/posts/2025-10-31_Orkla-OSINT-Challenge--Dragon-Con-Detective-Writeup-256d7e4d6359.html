Challenge 1: Locate Coca-Cola Freestyle Machine

Instructions:
 Find a Coca-Cola Freestyle machine near the Westin Peachtree Plaza in Atlanta, Georgia. Only the name of the restaurant or location is required.

Answer:
 Firehouse Subs, Peachtree Center Mall

Tools Used:

  • Google Dorking
  • Google Maps
  • Coca-Cola Freestyle location finder: https://www.coca-colafreestyle.com/find-freestyle-locations/#map:atlanta%7C5%7C

Methodology:
 Initially, I searched for Coca-Cola Freestyle machines near the client’s location using Google, but only two results appeared: one was a corporate office and the other a museum. To refine the search, I used Google dorking and the official Coca-Cola Freestyle website, which allowed me to locate a nearby restaurant offering the machine.

Challenge 2: Determine Secret Meet-Up Location

Instructions:
 Figure out where the subject went based on an audio clip. Only the name of the place is required.

Hint:
 Orkla: “3 words — outgoing, bongo, and skip.” These words correspond to a location.

Answer:
 Trader Vic’s, Atlanta

Tools Used:

  • Google Dorking
  • what3words

Methodology:
 The three words corresponded to a what3words geocoding system location. This system divides the Earth into 3m x 3m squares and assigns each a unique combination of three words. Searching the combination “///outgoing.bongo.skip” led directly to Trader Vic’s in Atlanta.

Challenge 3 & 4 : Identify Bronze Statue

Instructions:
 To the right of this picture, out of sight, there’s a bronze statue. Identify the name and its designer.

Answer:
 Ballet Olympia
 Designer: John C. Portman

Tools Used:

  • Reverse image search (Google)
  • Google Maps
  • Google dorking

Methodology:
 Atlanta has many statues, which initially caused some confusion. I combined information from the image, local events (Dragon Con parade route), and online resources to correctly identify the statue as Ballet Olympia. The designer was confirmed through further research.

Challenge 5: Confirm Identity of Boston Marathon Runner

Instructions:
 An insurance company suspects a client faked a knee injury. They attended the Boston Marathon between 2013–2018. Male, late 30s, from Hong Kong. Confirm the person’s name using a provided image.

Answer:
 Peter Yuen Man Ngai

Tools Used:

  • Google dorking
  • Reverse image search / face recognition
  • Marathon-specific photo database: MarathonFoto
  • Event reference: Thrillist Boston Marathon Guide

Methodology:
 I performed a reverse image search on the provided image. Noticing the runner’s bib and clothing year (2017), I used MarathonFoto to identify the individual. Cross-referencing multiple sources confirmed the full name.
